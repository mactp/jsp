Friends of Jacob Smith Park

About the Friends group, our work and the committee

Who are the Friends of JSP?

The Friends of Jacob Smith Park (FoJSP) is a Community Interest Group formed in 2008 (shortly after the park opened to the public) at the suggestion of the council. We’re volunteers dedicated to maintaining and enhancing the park so it remains a special place for everyone and supports a rich diversity of wildlife.

FoJSP works with North Yorkshire Council (NYC) who own the park and are responsible for core maintenance such as grass cutting, perimeter walls and tree management.

What we do

  • Represent the interests of the community and park users.
  • Agree annual priorities with NYC and help deliver the plan for the park.
  • Fundraise for improvements beyond the council’s responsibilities.
  • Arrange volunteering events to carry out agreed enhancements.
  • Hold public meetings twice a year to share progress and gather views.

Our guiding limits (as set out in Miss Jacob Smith’s will)

  • The park is a green space for public recreation only.
  • Preserve flora, fauna and wildlife.
  • Maintain the surrounding wall in good repair.
  • No new buildings to be erected.

Read our governance: FoJSP Constitution, Minutes of last public meeting, Minutes of last AGM.

FoJSP Committee

The FoJSP Committee is a relaxed, friendly group that meets a few times a year to agree priorities and oversee enhancements to the park.
