Get Involved

The Friends of Jacob Smith Park are all volunteers dedicated to keeping our park special for everyone — people, plants, and wildlife alike.

From maintaining oak benches and planting wildflowers, to pulling invasive balsam and creating wildlife habitats, our work helps ensure Jacob Smith Park remains a haven for nature and a peaceful place for visitors to enjoy. Whether you have a couple of hours to spare or can commit regularly, your time is always appreciated — and it really does make a difference.

Ongoing Care

We carry out annual oiling of the oak benches, hand pulling balsam in early summer, and general tidying. These regular tasks help preserve the park for everyone to enjoy.

One-off Projects

We also take on larger projects such as tree planting, sowing wildflowers, and adding new benches. These projects often involve local community groups, like the Cubs pictured here helping plant in our wildflower project.

Seasonal Work

From meadow raking to pond restoration, our seasonal work changes throughout the year, following the needs of the park and its wildlife.

Regular Volunteering Days

We meet on the first Wednesday of every month between March and October, from 10am to midday. Meet just inside the park gate — no booking required. Wear sturdy footwear and bring gloves if you have them. All tasks are optional, so you can choose what you’d like to get involved in.

Current Projects

Wildflowers Project

Launched in 2024, this project increases the range of native plants to support pollinators and wildlife. We’re planting species like yellow rattle, garlic mustard, red campion, knapweed, and birds-foot trefoil, alongside native shrubs such as alder buckthorn and spindle.

Boot Scraper

To keep paths and boots cleaner in winter, we’ll be installing a new boot scraper at the main entrance before winter 2025/26.

Balsam Management

Himalayan balsam is invasive and crowds out native species. We’re continuing control efforts with hoeing in spring and hand pulling in summer to prevent seed spread.

Recent Projects

  • Seven new oak benches added.
  • 12 new bat, owl and bird boxes installed.
  • Three-year programme to reduce balsam to manageable levels.
  • New information board at the park entrance.
  • Planted over 300 trees and 200 shrubs/plugs.
  • Hosted “Talks in the Park” with local experts.
  • Renovated ponds and replaced broken fencing around veteran trees.
