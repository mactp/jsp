Friends of Jacob Smith Park

Get Involved

Help us nurture, maintain and protect the park for future generations.

From oiling oak benches and planting wildflowers, to pulling invasive balsam and creating wildlife habitats, our work keeps Jacob Smith Park special. Whether you can spare a couple of hours or join us regularly, your time really does make a difference.

Ongoing Care

We carry out annual oiling of the oak benches, hand pulling balsam in early summer, and general tidying. These regular tasks help preserve the park for everyone to enjoy.

One-off Projects

We also take on larger projects such as tree planting, sowing wildflowers, and adding new benches. These often involve local community groups, like the Cubs pictured here planting for our wildflower project.

Seasonal Work

From meadow raking to pond restoration, our seasonal work follows the needs of the park and its wildlife throughout the year.

Regular Volunteering Days

We meet on the first Wednesday of every month, March to October, from 10am to midday. Meet just inside the gate — no booking required. Wear sturdy footwear and bring gloves if you can. All tasks are optional, so you can choose what you’d like to do.

Current Projects

Wildflowers Project

Launched in 2024, this project increases the range of native plants to support pollinators and wildlife. We’re planting species like yellow rattle, garlic mustard, red campion, knapweed and birds-foot trefoil, alongside native shrubs such as alder buckthorn and spindle.

Boot Scraper

To keep paths and boots cleaner in winter, we’ll be installing a new boot scraper at the main entrance before winter 2025/26.

Balsam Management

Himalayan balsam is invasive and crowds out native species. We’re continuing control efforts with hoeing in spring and hand pulling in summer to prevent seed spread.

Recent Projects

  • Seven new oak benches added.
  • 12 new bat, owl and bird boxes installed.
  • Three-year programme to reduce balsam to manageable levels.
  • New information board at the park entrance.
  • Planted over 300 trees and 200 shrubs/plugs.
  • Hosted “Talks in the Park” with local experts.
  • Renovated ponds and replaced broken fencing around veteran trees.
