History of the Family Who Granted Us the Freedom of the Park

Throughout every season, Jacob Smith Park offers its treasures for us to share and enjoy. We will be forever grateful as a community to Winifred Jacob Smith MBE for her wonderful bequest which, in her own words, is:

“For people to enjoy the freedom and the beauty that public parks bring.”

— Winifred Jacob Smith MBE

Winifred Jacob Smith MBE

Winifred was born on 4 August 1911 to Dora and Jacob Smith, a farming family whose roots stretch back to 1710 at Humberton between Knaresborough and Boroughbridge, including farms on the Newby Hall Estate at Givendale and at Burton Grange.

Traditionally, the first son in each generation was named Jacob. With daughters Winifred and Dorothy (born 18 May 1910), their father adopted the name into the family surname so the tradition could live on. Dora, president of the Knaresborough Women’s Institute (WI), and both daughters were practical, community-minded women who played leading roles in the farm and local life.

Service, Community & Legacy

Educated at St Margaret’s School (Scarborough) and later at Yorkshire Ladies’ College (Leeds), Winifred became a cornerstone of the Women’s Land Army (WLA) during the Second World War. She prioritised the welfare of land girls across North Yorkshire and later the whole of Yorkshire, keeping detailed diaries and editing the monthly WLA Bulletin. In recognition of her service, she was awarded the MBE on 28 February 1951 by King George VI.

Winifred and her sister Dorothy also managed the family dairy business at Park Corner Farm, Scriven, with a pedigree Ayrshire herd, following the death of their father in December 1941. In 1965 the sisters purchased the 30 acres of Scriven Park they had previously leased, which later became Jacob Smith Park.

Jacob Smith Family — Timeline

  1. 18 May 1910

    Dorothy Smith is born.

  2. 4 August 1911

    Winifred Smith is born.

  3. c.1920 (post-WWI)

    The family moves to Knaresborough; Jacob takes over Park Corner Farm, Scriven.

  4. 1937

    At the WI, Winifred meets Lady Celia Coates, who asks her to “look after the Women’s Land Army if war should break out”.

  5. 1938

    Winifred & Dorothy publish a book of local recipes to raise funds for the parish church.

  6. Sept 1939 – 1945

    Winifred joins the WLA and becomes organiser for North Yorkshire, later all Yorkshire — keeping unique diaries and editing the WLA Bulletin.

  7. Dec 1941

    Jacob (father) dies aged 74; Winifred & Dorothy run the dairy enterprise with their Ayrshire herd.

  8. 28 Feb 1951

    Winifred is awarded the MBE at Buckingham Palace.

  9. 1965

    Winifred & Dorothy purchase 30 acres of Scriven Park (previously leased) for grazing.

  10. 2 May 1974

    Dora Smith dies, aged 100.

  11. 26 April 1984

    Dorothy Jacob Smith dies, aged 73.

  12. 23 May 2003

    Winifred Jacob Smith MBE dies, aged 91. Wartime memorabilia donated to Yorkshire Farming Museum; major legacy to Yorkshire Cancer Research. Park bequeathed for public enjoyment.

  13. January 2008

    Jacob Smith Park officially opens to the public.

  14. 2010

    Somerley, the family home, is demolished after falling into disrepair. Somerley Lane now marks the site.

Knaresborough Post — Archive Extracts

16 March 1940 — WLA reception by the Queen

Miss Winifred Jacob Smith … selected as one of the Women’s Land Army representatives to attend a reception by the Queen in London. Miss Smith has been acting as secretary for the North Riding branch of the Land Army.

15 June 1940 — Wartime cookery lectures

Miss Dorothy Jacob Smith delivers free wartime cookery classes to packed audiences at the WI and the Modern School, demonstrating nourishing dishes without rationed foods.

12 October 1940 — School potato-picking

Pupils assist local farmers with the potato harvest at Scriven and Hopewell House; Mr Jacob Smith praises their efforts.

10 May 1941 — Services Club & Canteen

Miss Dorothy Jacob Smith appointed assistant organiser; committee funds support local regiments.
