Quick facts

Iron Age traces

Guiseley Hill at the top of the park is a possible Iron Age settlement with bank-and-ditch earthworks and traces of hut circles. No excavation has taken place.

Medieval ridge & furrow

Historic ploughing patterns are visible when the grass is short — classic signs of medieval arable farming.

Estate landscaping

Low Wood was planted as part of 18th-century landscaping by the Slingsby family, who also built the estate wall.

WWII role

Scriven Hall was used as an army camp; the parkland trained tank crews and infantry.

Public park

Bequeathed by Winifred Jacob Smith MBE for public enjoyment; opened in 2008.

Frequently asked (short!)

Can I see the Iron Age remains?

There are no formal excavations or interpretation boards yet. Subtle earthworks are visible on Guiseley Hill; please keep to paths and avoid disturbing features.

Where can I learn more about Winifred?

See the family history page and the sources below; the Yorkshire Farming Museum holds WLA memorabilia donated from Somerley.

Are the ancient trees protected?

Many veteran oaks are recorded in the Woodland Trust Ancient Tree Inventory. Fencing protects roots and understory; please don’t climb on them.
