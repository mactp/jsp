Planning Your Visit

Whether it’s your first time or your hundredth, here’s everything you need to enjoy your time at Jacob Smith Park.

Where are we?

Entrance:
Scriven Road, Scriven, Knaresborough HG5 9EJ
(at the junction with Park Grove)

What3Words: milder.suppose.plump

  • Parking: Street parking is very limited on Scriven Road. Police Community Support Officers regularly patrol the area.
  • Nearest toilets: Conyngham Hall, Knaresborough HG5 9AY — approximately a 20-minute walk or 5-minute drive.

Inside Jacob Smith Park

Jacob Smith Park has just one entrance. It is enclosed but there may be gaps in some perimeter fencing, so you should only let dogs off the lead if they are under control, in your sight and will come when called. Please ensure you clean up after your dog and dispose of waste in the bin near the park entrance.

The park is a managed wild space with a network of paths criss-crossing its 30 acres and has a perimeter walk covering approximately three-quarters of a mile. Some of the paths involve steeper climbs but these can be avoided by using other paths.

There are seven benches at various points around the park to rest or take in the beautiful views, which extend as far as the White Horse on the North York Moors. There is also a small recreational area near to the entrance for children to run around, play ball games and fly kites.

Note: some paths, especially around the entrance, can be very muddy in winter or after heavy rain.

What to Enjoy

  • Nature Trails: Discover winding paths through woodland and wildflower meadows.
  • Wildlife Watching: Visit our Wildlife page to learn what to look for.
  • Picnic Spots: Enjoy peaceful moments with friends or family.
  • History Walks: Learn more about our fascinating past on the History page.
  • Community Events: Check out what’s on or get involved yourself.

Wildlife

There is a range of habitats throughout the park, including woods, meadow and grasslands, wetlands and veteran trees. These habitats support a rich and diverse variety of wildlife.

Children are encouraged to explore the park with a guided nature trail; this has a beautifully illustrated guide with notes to help children discover an exciting new world.
