Wildlife and Plant Life

Jacob Smith Park is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna. Learn more about what lives and grows here, and how we protect it for future generations.

What You’ll Find

  • Birds: Spot woodpeckers, owls, sparrowhawks, and seasonal migrants like swallows and warblers.
  • Mammals: Foxes, badgers, hedgehogs and even deer are occasionally seen.
  • Insects: Butterflies, dragonflies, and rare species of beetle thrive in our meadows.
  • Wildflowers: A carpet of bluebells in spring and a vibrant mix of native species in summer.
  • Trees & Shrubs: Ancient oaks, silver birch, and areas of rewilded woodland support biodiversity.

How We Care for the Environment

The Friends of Jacob Smith Park work with local ecologists and volunteers to maintain the delicate balance of nature within the park. Our efforts include:

  • Seasonal surveys to monitor wildlife populations and plant diversity
  • Habitat restoration, such as pond clearance and wildflower re-seeding
  • Careful path management to reduce human impact on sensitive areas
  • Bird and bat box installation to support nesting and roosting
  • Invasive species removal to protect native ecosystems

How You Can Help

Visitors play a vital role in helping us protect our park’s wildlife. Please:

  • Keep to marked paths and avoid disturbing nesting areas
  • Don’t pick wildflowers or disturb logs and habitats
  • Observe wildlife quietly and from a distance
  • Dispose of waste responsibly or take it home

Seasonal Highlights

Each season offers something new:

  • Spring: Bluebells, birdsong, and frogspawn in ponds
  • Summer: Butterflies and wildflower meadows
  • Autumn: Fungi and migrating birds
  • Winter: Bare tree silhouettes and signs of mammals in the frost
